  • BBC micro:bit

The micro:bit doesn't have a built-in speaker! To hear sounds, you'll need to connect headphones or a speaker to the micro:bit's pins, or use the simulator which can play sounds. For this session, we'll focus on creating the code - you can test it in the simulator!

üéµ What is Music on micro:bit?

The micro:bit can play musical notes and melodies! Music is made up of notes (like C, D, E) and rhythms (how long each note plays). You can play individual notes or entire songs!

üöÄ Activity 1: Play a Single Note

Step 1: Open MakeCode and create a new project called "Music"
Step 2: Go to the "Music" category (you might need to add it - click the gear icon and add "Music")
Step 3: Find the "play tone" block
Step 4: Add "on button A pressed" and put "play tone" inside it
Step 5: Click on the note (Middle C) and choose a different note
Step 6: Test in the simulator - click button A to hear the note!
Musical notes are named with letters (C, D, E, F, G, A, B) and numbers (like Middle C, High C). Higher numbers = higher pitch!

üé® Activity 2: Play a Melody

Step 1: Find the "play melody" block in the Music category
Step 2: Add it to "on button A pressed"
Step 3: Click on the melody name and choose a built-in tune like "Ba Ding" or "Entertainer"
Step 4: Test it - you'll hear a full song!

üéØ Activity 3: Create Your Own Tune

Step 1: Use multiple "play tone" blocks in sequence
Step 2: Create a simple tune like "Happy Birthday" or "Twinkle Twinkle"
Step 3: Add "pause" blocks between notes to control the rhythm
Step 4: Try different notes to create your own melody!

üåü Activity 4: Sound Effects

Step 1: Create a new project called "SoundEffects"
Step 2: Add "on button A pressed" with a high-pitched beep
Step 3: Add "on button B pressed" with a low-pitched beep
Step 4: Add "on shake" with a fun sound effect
Step 5: Try using "ring tone" for different sound effects!

üéπ Activity 5: Musical Instrument

Step 1: Create a program where different buttons play different notes
Step 2: Button A plays one note, Button B plays another
Step 3: Try tilting to play different notes too!
Create a musical program that:
  • Plays a different note for each button (A, B, A+B)
  • Plays a melody when shaken
  • Shows an icon on screen while playing music
  • Hint: Use "show icon" blocks with your music blocks!

üí° Key Concepts

  • Note: A single musical sound with a specific pitch
  • Melody: A sequence of notes that make a tune
  • Pitch: How high or low a note sounds
  • Tone: A musical note with a specific frequency
  • Rhythm: The timing and duration of notes
