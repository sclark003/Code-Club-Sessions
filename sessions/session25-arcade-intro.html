üéÆ What is an Arcade Console?

The Arcade console is a special attachment for your micro:bit that turns it into a handheld game console! It has buttons, a color screen, and lets you create and play games. We'll use MakeCode Arcade to program games - it's similar to regular MakeCode but designed for making games!

MakeCode Arcade is different from regular MakeCode for micro:bit. Make sure you're using arcade.makecode.com, not makecode.microbit.org!

üöÄ Activity 1: Set Up MakeCode Arcade

Step 1: Go to arcade.makecode.com
Step 2: Click on "New Project" and name it "My First Game"
Step 3: Look at the screen - you'll see a game simulator on the left and code blocks on the right
Step 4: Notice the blocks are different from regular MakeCode - these are for making games!
Step 5: Click the gear icon (‚öôÔ∏è) and select your Arcade console type (Kitronik Arcade, Elecfreaks, etc.)

üé® Activity 2: Create Your First Sprite

Step 1: Look for the "Sprites" category in the blocks menu
Step 2: Find the "set mySprite to sprite of kind Player" block
Step 3: Drag it into the "on start" block (which is already on your screen)
Step 4: Click on the image in the block - you'll see a sprite editor open!
Step 5: Choose a sprite from the gallery or draw your own!
Step 6: Look at the simulator - you should see your sprite appear on the screen!
A sprite is a character or object in your game that can move around. You can have many sprites in one game!

üéØ Activity 3: Position Your Sprite

Step 1: Find the "set mySprite position to x 80 y 60" block (in Sprites category)
Step 2: Add it after your sprite creation block
Step 3: Change the x and y numbers to move your sprite to different positions
Step 4: The screen is 160 pixels wide (x) and 120 pixels tall (y)
Step 5: Try different positions: x=0 (left), x=160 (right), y=0 (top), y=120 (bottom)

üåü Activity 4: Multiple Sprites

Step 1: Create a second sprite by adding another "set mySprite to sprite" block
Step 2: Change the name from "mySprite" to "enemy" (click on the name)
Step 3: Choose a different sprite image (maybe a monster or obstacle)
Step 4: Set its position to a different location
Step 5: Now you have two sprites on screen!
Create a scene with:
  • A player sprite (you!)
  • An enemy sprite
  • A collectible item sprite
  • Position them in different parts of the screen
  • Use different colors and images for each!

üí° Key Concepts

  • Arcade Console: A hardware attachment that turns micro:bit into a game console
  • MakeCode Arcade: A special version of MakeCode designed for making games
  • Sprite: A character or object in your game that can move and interact
  • Coordinates: X and Y positions that tell you where something is on the screen
  • Pixel: A tiny dot of color - the screen is made of many pixels
  • Screen Size: 160 pixels wide (x) by 120 pixels tall (y)
