üöÄ Activity 1: Button A

Step 1: Open MakeCode and start a new project called "Buttons"
Step 2: Look in the "Input" category. Find the "on button A pressed" block
Step 3: Drag this block onto your workspace. Notice it's different from "on start" - it waits for something to happen!
Step 4: From the "Basic" category, drag a "show string" block inside the "on button A pressed" block
Step 5: Change the text to "Hello!" or your favorite message
Step 6: Click button A in the simulator - watch your message appear!
Step 7: Download and test on your real micro:bit. Press button A and see what happens!
The micro:bit has two buttons: Button A (on the left) and Button B (on the right). You can also press both together!

üé® Activity 2: Button B

Step 1: Add an "on button B pressed" block to your program
Step 2: Add a "show icon" block inside it - choose a different icon (maybe a star or a duck!)
Step 3: Test it! Button A shows text, Button B shows an icon

üéØ Activity 3: Both Buttons Together

Step 1: Add an "on button A+B pressed" block
Step 2: Add a "show animation" block from the Basic category
Step 3: Choose a fun animation like "blink" or "heartbeat"
Step 4: Test it by pressing both buttons at the same time!
Create a program where:
  • Button A shows your name
  • Button B shows your age
  • Both buttons together show a celebration animation!

üí° Key Concepts

  • Input: When you press a button, that's "input" - information going INTO your program
  • Event: Pressing a button is an "event" - something that happens that your program can respond to
  • Event Handler: Blocks like "on button A pressed" are called "event handlers" - they wait for events and then do something
  • Conditional: The program checks "IF button A is pressed, THEN do this" - this is conditional logic!
