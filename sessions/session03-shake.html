üî¨ What is an Accelerometer?

The micro:bit has a built-in accelerometer - a sensor that can detect movement, shaking, and which way is up! It's like a tiny motion detector inside your micro:bit.

üöÄ Activity 1: Shake Detector

Step 1: Open MakeCode and create a new project called "Shake"
Step 2: Go to the "Input" category and find "on shake"
Step 3: Drag the "on shake" block onto your workspace
Step 4: Add a "show icon" block inside it - choose a fun icon like a star or diamond
Step 5: In the simulator, click the "shake" button to test it
Step 6: Download to your micro:bit and shake it - watch the icon appear!
The accelerometer detects when the micro:bit moves quickly in any direction. Try shaking it gently, then harder - it should work both ways!

üé® Activity 2: Tilt Detection

Step 1: Add a new "on tilt left" block from the Input category
Step 2: Add a "show string" block that says "LEFT"
Step 3: Add an "on tilt right" block with "RIGHT"
Step 4: Try adding "on tilt forward" and "on tilt backward" too!
Step 5: Test by tilting your micro:bit in different directions

üéØ Activity 3: Dice Roller

Step 1: Create a new project called "Dice"
Step 2: Add an "on shake" block
Step 3: Go to the "Math" category and find "pick random"
Step 4: Drag "pick random" into a "show number" block (from Basic category)
Step 5: Change the numbers in "pick random" to 1 and 6 (for a dice!)
Step 6: Shake your micro:bit to roll the dice!
Can you make a magic 8-ball? Shake the micro:bit and it shows a random answer like "Yes", "No", "Maybe", or "Try again"?

üí° Key Concepts

  • Sensor: A device that detects something in the real world (like movement)
  • Accelerometer: A sensor that measures acceleration and movement
  • Random: When something happens by chance - like rolling a dice!
  • Event: Shaking or tilting are events, just like button presses
