üöÄ Activity 1: Your First Program

Step 1: Go to makecode.microbit.org
Step 2: Click on "New Project" and give it a name like "Hello World"
Step 3: Look at the screen. On the left is a micro:bit simulator, in the middle are code blocks, and on the right is your workspace.
Step 4: Find the "on start" block (it's already on your screen). This block runs once when the micro:bit turns on.
Step 5: Look in the "Basic" category. Find the "show string" block and drag it inside the "on start" block.
Step 6: Click on the word "Hello" in the block and change it to your name!
Step 7: Watch the simulator on the left - you should see your name scrolling across the screen!
Step 8: Connect your micro:bit to your computer with the USB cable. Click the "Download" button at the bottom of the screen.
Step 9: Drag the downloaded file onto your micro:bit (it will appear like a USB drive). Your program will now run on the real micro:bit!
The micro:bit has a 5x5 grid of LEDs (25 lights total). Text scrolls across this grid one letter at a time.

üé® Activity 2: Display Images

Step 1: Find the "show icon" block in the Basic category
Step 2: Drag it into your "on start" block (below your "show string" block)
Step 3: Click on the heart icon and choose a different image from the menu (try a smiley face!)
Step 4: Download and test on your micro:bit
Can you make your micro:bit show your name, then a happy face, then your name again? Try adding more "show string" and "show icon" blocks!

üí° Key Concepts

  • Output: When your program displays something (like text or images), that's called "output"
  • Blocks: In MakeCode, we build programs by connecting blocks together
  • On Start: This block runs automatically when the micro:bit turns on
  • LEDs: Light Emitting Diodes - the tiny lights that make up the micro:bit screen
