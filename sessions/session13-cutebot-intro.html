ü§ñ What is Cutebot?

Cutebot is a smart robot car that you can program with your micro:bit! It has wheels, motors, lights, and sensors. You attach your micro:bit to the Cutebot, and then you can make it drive around, follow lines, avoid obstacles, and more!

Make sure your Cutebot is assembled and the micro:bit is securely attached before starting!

üöÄ Activity 1: Add Cutebot Extension

Step 1: Go to makecode.microbit.org
Step 2: Click on "New Project" and name it "Cutebot Intro"
Step 3: Click on the gear icon (‚öôÔ∏è) in the top right, then click "Extensions"
Step 4: Search for "cutebot" and click on the "cutebot" extension by ElecFreaks
Step 5: You'll see a new category appear called "Cutebot" with blocks for controlling your robot!
If you can't find the extension, try this link: Cutebot Extension

üé® Activity 2: Make Cutebot Move Forward

Step 1: Look in the "Cutebot" category - you'll see blocks for controlling the robot
Step 2: Find the "move forward at speed %" block
Step 3: Drag it into the "on start" block
Step 4: Set the speed to 50 (half speed - good for testing!)
Step 5: Add a "pause" block (from Basic) set to 2000ms (2 seconds)
Step 6: Add a "stop" block (from Cutebot category) to make it stop after 2 seconds
Step 7: Download the code to your micro:bit and attach it to Cutebot
Step 8: Turn on Cutebot and watch it move forward for 2 seconds!
Speed values range from 0 (stopped) to 100 (full speed). Start with lower speeds (30-50) to be safe!

üéØ Activity 3: Different Movements

Step 1: Create a new program that makes Cutebot:
  • Move forward for 2 seconds
  • Stop for 1 second
  • Move backward for 2 seconds
  • Stop
Step 2: Use "move backward at speed %" for going backwards
Step 3: Test it and watch Cutebot move!

üåü Activity 4: Turning

Step 1: Find the "turn left at speed %" and "turn right at speed %" blocks
Step 2: Create a program that:
  • Moves forward for 1 second
  • Turns left for 1 second
  • Moves forward for 1 second
  • Turns right for 1 second
  • Stops
Step 3: Test it - Cutebot should drive in a square pattern!
Create a program that makes Cutebot drive in a figure-8 pattern! You'll need to combine forward movement with left and right turns.

üí° Key Concepts

  • Extension: Extra blocks added to MakeCode for specific devices (like Cutebot)
  • Motor Control: Telling the robot's wheels how fast to spin and in which direction
  • Speed: How fast the robot moves (0-100, where 100 is maximum speed)
  • Forward/Backward: Direction of movement
  • Turn: Rotating the robot left or right
  • Stop: Stopping all movement
