üé® Game Ideas

Here are some ideas to get you started. Feel free to come up with your own or modify these!

Option 1: Collect the Coins

  • Player moves around collecting coins
  • Avoid enemies that move around
  • Score points for each coin collected
  • Game over if you touch an enemy
  • Win when you collect all coins
  • Multiple levels with increasing difficulty

Option 2: Platform Jumper

  • Player jumps between platforms
  • Collect items while jumping
  • Avoid falling off the screen
  • Score points for items collected
  • Enemies move on platforms
  • Multiple levels with different platform layouts

Option 3: Space Shooter

  • Player spaceship at bottom of screen
  • Enemies fall from top
  • Shoot enemies with button A
  • Score points for each enemy destroyed
  • Game over if enemy reaches bottom
  • Power-ups appear occasionally

Option 4: Maze Runner

  • Navigate through a maze
  • Collect items in the maze
  • Reach the exit to complete level
  • Avoid enemies patrolling the maze
  • Multiple maze levels
  • Timer adds challenge

Option 5: Racing Game

  • Car moves left and right
  • Avoid obstacles coming toward you
  • Collect power-ups
  • Score based on distance traveled
  • Speed increases over time
  • Game over on collision

Option 6: Your Own Idea!

Think about what kind of game you'd like to make. What's fun? What would be challenging? What would you want to play?

üìù Planning Your Game

Step 1: Choose your game idea
Step 2: Plan your game:
  • What is the player's goal?
  • How does the player move/control?
  • What are the obstacles or enemies?
  • How does scoring work?
  • What are the win/lose conditions?
  • Will there be multiple levels?
  • What does it look like (background, sprites)?
  • What sounds will it have?
Step 3: Draw or write down your plan
Step 4: Start building one feature at a time:
  • First: Player movement
  • Then: Basic gameplay
  • Then: Scoring
  • Then: Enemies/obstacles
  • Then: Polish (animation, sound, backgrounds)
Step 5: Test each feature as you add it
Step 6: Combine everything and test the complete game

üîß Tips for Success

Start simple! Get the basic gameplay working first, then add features one at a time.
Test often! Play your game after each new feature to make sure it's fun and works correctly.
Use comments! Add comment blocks to explain what your code does - it helps you remember later!
Debug step by step! If something doesn't work, test each part separately to find the problem.
Make it fun! Add your own creative touches - unique sprites, sounds, or game mechanics!
Ask for feedback! Let others play your game and see what they think!
